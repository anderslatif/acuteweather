e (vapor pressure hPa) = (RH / 100) × 6.105 × exp((17.27 × T) / (237.7 + T)) =

This calculates the vapor pressure ein hPa, based on temperature and humidity.

AT (Apparent Temperature) = T + 0.33 × e − 0.70 × wind − 4.00 =

0.33 boosts humidity’s warmth effect, 0.70 scales wind’s cooling, and 4.00 adjusts the baseline.

They are calibration constants based on the Apparent Temperature formula.

FeelsLike = AT + R contribution − P contribution =

My formula additionally takes solar radiation (R) and precipitation (P) into account.
